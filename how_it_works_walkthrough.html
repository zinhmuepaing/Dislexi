Homework help that reads the rules, then teaches the word.

During national exams, students with dyslexia get a human reader who reads questions exactly as written. At home, they get nothing. Our system puts that reader — and a tutor, and a phonics coach — on the phone the family already owns.

Live highlight, synced to the voice

The setup: a stand, a mirror, zero extra computers

The phone sits in an ordinary stand, screen facing the student. A small mirror clip bends the front camera's view straight down onto the worksheet — so the paper stays in front of the child, exactly like a real exam desk.
